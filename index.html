About Us

  • About
  • Mission
  • Vision
The Health Promotion Foundation (HPF) is a non-profit non-governmental organization established in 2023 AD, in Chitwan District. with a aim of creating a healthy society where every individual will have the knowledge, resources and support that they need to live a healthy and prosperous life. The organization is dedicated to empowering individuals to make positive choices in their lives to prevent illness and promote their health. HPF is always working to promote health and wellness through education, advocacy and community-building initiatives.
Our mission is to empower individuals and communities to take control of their health and well-being through education, advocacy, and support. We believe that everyone has the right to live a healthy life, and we are committed to promoting health equity and social justice. Our goal is to create a world where health is a priority for all, and where individuals have the knowledge, resources, and support they need to make healthy choices.
Our vision is to create a world where health and well-being are prioritized for all individuals and communities. We envision a future where everyone has access to the resources and support they need to live healthy, fulfilling lives. We believe that by promoting health equity and social justice, we can create a more just and equitable society where everyone has the opportunity to thrive.

Our Activities

We believe we can make a difference

Prevention of Disease and Promotion of Health

We focus on reducing risk factors, encouraging healthy lifestyles, and creating supportive environments.

Lifestyle Modification

Lifestyle modification involves making positive changes in daily habits such as eating a balanced diet, engaging in regular physical activity, managing stress, ensuring adequate sleep, and avoiding harmful practices like smoking or excessive alcohol consumption.

Climate change and agricultural health

We focuses on safe and sustainable farming practices, ensuring food security, reducing the spread of pests and diseases, and protecting both farmers’ and consumers’ health.”

Awareness and Health Education

Non-communicable diseases such as heart disease, diabetes, cancer, and chronic respiratory illnesses are major public health challenges. Activities to address NCDs include health education, lifestyle modification, regular screening, early diagnosis, promoting healthy diets, encouraging physical activity, controlling tobacco and alcohol use, and ensuring access to quality care and medicines.
